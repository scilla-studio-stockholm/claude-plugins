## Core Exploration: The Timeline (25-30 min)

*Goal: Reconstruct the JTBD timeline—first thought through decision.*

### First Thought / Struggling Moment (8-10 min)

**Q3:** Take me back to when you first started thinking about [SOLVING THIS PROBLEM / FINDING A SOLUTION FOR X]. What was going on at that time?

*Listen for: Specific trigger, circumstances, timeline*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If vague on timing: "Can you remember roughly when this was? What else was happening at work/in your life?"
- → If they jump to solution: "Back up a bit—before you found [solution], what was the situation?"
- → If they give general answer: "Can you think of a specific moment or incident that made you realize this?"

**Q4:** What wasn't working about how you were handling it before?

*Listen for: Functional pain, emotional pain, social pain*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If functional only: "How did that make you feel when [problem] happened?"
- → If they mention others: "How did [person/team] react to this?"
- → If mild frustration: "What was the worst instance of this you can remember?"

**Q5:** If nothing had changed, what would have happened?

*Listen for: Stakes, urgency, consequences*

---

### Passive → Active Looking (7-8 min)

**Q6:** Before you started seriously looking for a solution, what options were you already aware of?

*Listen for: Awareness channels, initial impressions, misconceptions*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If they knew of options: "What did you think of those at the time? Why hadn't you tried them yet?"
- → If unaware: "Where did you first hear about solutions for this kind of problem?"

**Q7:** What happened that made you actually start looking seriously?

*Listen for: Trigger event, breaking point, external pressure*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If external trigger: "Tell me more about [trigger]. What specifically happened?"
- → If gradual: "Was there a last straw moment, or did it just build up?"
- → If someone else pushed: "What did [person] say or do that made you act?"

---

### Deciding (7-8 min)

**Q8:** What options did you seriously consider?

*Listen for: Competitive set, evaluation criteria*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If only one option: "Did you consider doing nothing or finding a workaround?"
- → If many options: "How did you narrow it down?"

**Q9:** How did you evaluate them? What mattered most to you?

*Listen for: Criteria—functional, emotional, social*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If feature-focused: "Beyond the features, what else mattered?"
- → If price-focused: "If price were the same, how would you have decided?"

**Q10:** What concerns did you have about [chosen solution]?

*Listen for: Anxieties, risks, switching costs*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If no concerns: "Was there anything you were giving up by switching?"
- → If concerns: "How did you get past that concern?"

**Q11:** What made you ultimately decide?

*Listen for: Tipping point, final push*

---

### Consuming / Outcomes (5-7 min)

**Q12:** Now that you've been using it, has it delivered what you hoped?

*Listen for: Expectations vs reality, progress made*

**Conditional follow-ups:**
- → If yes: "What's the biggest difference compared to before?"
- → If mixed: "What's working and what's falling short?"
- → If no: "What did you expect that hasn't happened?"

**Q13:** What surprised you—good or bad?

*Listen for: Unexpected value, hidden problems, jobs-to-be-done unlocked*

**Q14:** Is there anything it hasn't solved that you hoped it would?

*Listen for: Remaining pain, expansion opportunities, churn risk*

## Researcher Notes

### What to Listen For

**Struggling moment signals:**
- Emotional language (frustrated, embarrassed, worried, stuck)
- Specific incidents with details
- Other people involved
- Stakes/consequences mentioned

**Forces of progress:**
- **Push:** Pain with status quo, things getting worse
- **Pull:** Vision of better future, specific outcomes desired
- **Anxiety:** Concerns about switching, fear of unknown
- **Habit:** Comfort with current way, "good enough"

**Red flags in responses:**
- Speaking in generalities (need to anchor to specifics)
- Jumping to features (need to back up to context)
- No emotion in the story (need to probe feelings)
- Perfect recall (may be post-hoc rationalization)
