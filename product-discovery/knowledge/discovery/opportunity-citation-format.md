# Opportunity citation format

## The format — what a citation contains

Each opportunity citation contains:

- **The quote** — verbatim from the interview
- **Source** — who said it + interview or video reference + approximate timestamp
- **Tweaks** marked with [square brackets]

Example of a complete citation:

> "That [interruption in the onboarding flow] becomes so annoying when it happens in the middle of [registration]"
>
> Frans, interview 4, ~12 min

## When to tweak

Most often when one of the following applies:

- **Deictic language** — "that thing", "the one we talked about". Words that only work in conversational context.
- **References to a prior question or answer** — "yes exactly like you said", "no, the first option", "as I mentioned".
- **Internal terms or abbreviations** — terms that only the interviewee or interviewer knows the meaning of.
- **Long, drawn-out quotes** — can be shortened with [...] if what's cut doesn't carry the nuance.

## When NOT to tweak

- **When the customer's wording carries the nuance.** A surprising or unusual expression is often the most valuable. Preserve it.
- **When you're unsure of the intention.** Tweaks assume you know what the person meant. If you don't know, ask the trio or leave it raw.
- **To clean up the quote.** Pauses, "like", "you know", grammatical mistakes — preserve them. They are the customer's voice.

## Rules for tweaks

- Square brackets around everything added or replaced
- Preserve the intention of the quote; don't replace it
- When you cut something out, mark with [...] so it's visible
- If unsure: write two versions, original and tweaked, and discuss in a joint review

## Quick rule

If you doubt whether a tweak is OK, ask yourself: *would the customer recognize themselves in this quote if they read it?* If yes, probably OK. If no, you've paraphrased.

## Examples to tweak together

Generic examples that illustrate the patterns. Substitute with real raw quotes from your interviews if you have them.

### Example 1 — deictic language

**Raw:**
> "Yeah exactly, that's what I mean, that thing becomes so annoying when it happens in the middle"

Discussion questions: what is "that thing"? What is "in the middle"?

**Possible tweak:**
> "That [interruption] becomes so annoying when it happens in the middle of [registration]"

### Example 2 — reference to a prior question

**Raw:**
> "Yeah well, like you said, it's not really what I need"

Discussion questions: what did the interviewer refer to? How do we preserve that without paraphrasing the customer?

**Possible tweak:**
> "[The suggested auto-complete] is not really what I need"

### Example 3 — internal term

**Raw:**
> "We just run our POC and hope it holds up"

Discussion questions: is POC clear to everyone on the team who'll read the citation? Should it be explained?

**Possible tweak** (if POC isn't obvious to the reader):
> "We just run our POC [proof-of-concept] and hope it holds up"

Or: keep it raw if the whole team talks POC daily.

### Example 4 — long quote

**Raw:**
> "Yeah so when I sat down to do that thing I first thought this is pretty easy but then I started wondering if it was really the right data I was looking at and then I got unsure and then I wanted to like double-check and then there was no good way to do that"

Discussion questions: what's the core? What can be cut without losing nuance?

**Possible tweak:**
> "When I [had to do it] I first thought this is pretty easy but then [...] I got unsure [...] and there was no good way to [double-check the data]"
