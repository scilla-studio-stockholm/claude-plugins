# Case Study Writing (Fallstudie för scilla.studio)

Write case studies that show real impact - concrete, honest, showing real change. Not fluff.

## Tone and Voice (CRITICAL)

- **Direct and professional** - never stiff or corporate
- **Concrete over abstract** - focus on what actually happened
- **Humble but confident** - acknowledge complexity
- **Practical** - tools and methods are for using, not admiring
- **Storytelling** - data tells a story, not the other way around
- **Subtly humorous** - don't take ourselves too seriously

### Language Markers

✅ **Use:**
- "på riktigt", "faktiskt", "konkret"
- "Det är så man bygger X: inte genom Y, utan genom Z"
- Direct participant voices as quotes
- Natural Swedish expressions

❌ **Avoid:**
- Buzzwords without substance ("transformation", "disruptiv", "strategisk partner")
- Vague formulations ("ökad förståelse", "förbättrad process")
- Excessive positivity
- Passive constructions when active works better

### Example of Right Tone

> "Utbildningen blev en katalysator för Avanzas förflyttning från Product Owner till Product Manager. Den skapade en gemensam förståelse för hur produktteam driver affärsvärde och gav varje PM konkreta verktyg för att tänka, kommunicera och agera mer strategiskt."

## Structure

### 1. Executive Summary (4-6 sentences)
- Client + brief description
- Assignment (number of participants, scope)
- Purpose in one sentence
- Quantitative results (NPS, rating)
- Key effect - what PMs actually got

**End with:** One sentence summarizing essence and value

**Template:**
```
**Kund:** [Company name]
**Uppdrag:** [What + scope]
**Syfte:** [Why - the strategic shift]
**Resultat:** [Quantitative - NPS, rating]
**Nyckeleffekt:** [What participants concretely got - tools and capabilities]

[Summary sentence about catalyst effect and essence]
```

### 2. Background & Challenge (3-4 paragraphs)

**Tell:**
- Where did client stand strategically? What shift were they making?
- What common pattern did leadership see in their PMs?
- What specific capabilities were missing?
- Why did scilla.studio's training become relevant right now?

**Structure:**
1. Strategic context (where are they, where going)
2. Common pattern/challenge leadership saw
3. Concrete missing capabilities (bullet points)
4. Why scilla.studio became the tool for this shift

### 3. Setup and Method (2-3 paragraphs)

**Describe:**
- Format (days, spacing, assignments)
- Pedagogical principle (how do you learn this?)
- What makes the setup special

**Structure:**
```markdown
**Format:**
- [Number of days, structure]
- [Homework and feedback loop]
- [Mix of methods]
- [All work based on participants' real cases]

**Pedagogisk princip:**
[1-2 sentences about how we believe you learn product leadership]
```

### 4. Focus Areas: [Number] Key Capabilities (1 paragraph intro + 4-6 areas)

Describe capabilities the training built. For each:
1. **Capability Name**
   What participants did to practice it

**End with:** An overarching theme tying capabilities together

**Example:**
> Genomgående tema: **trianguleringen mellan strategi, problemkartläggning och prioritering** - inte som separata steg, utan som en kontinuerlig process.

### 5. Results and Impact

#### Quantitative Results
Bullet points with concrete numbers:
- NPS
- Instructor ratings
- % who experienced X

#### Qualitative Results
- What participants described they got
- Most appreciated moments (bullet points)
- 3-5 participant quotes showing insight-moments

**Quote Guide:**
- Choose quotes showing concrete change in thinking
- Vary between "aha-moments" and application
- Include quotes showing complexity, not just praise

#### Organizational Impact
- Quotes from client/leadership
- Concrete examples of how work changed after training

### 6. [Optional Section - if needed]
Add if there's something unique to this project:
- Special challenges handled
- Follow-up and continued work
- Strategic impact at company level

## Good Formulations Examples

**About the challenge:**
> "Teamen var starka på att leverera funktionalitet, men svagare på att formulera _varför_ de byggde det de byggde, och vilket affärsvärde det faktiskt skapade."

**About method:**
> "Fokus låg på att omsätta teori till praktik, direkt i deltagarnas egna produktområden."

**About pedagogy:**
> "Product Management är komplext och en av de mest utmanande rollerna man kan ha. Ingen har alla svar, men vi hjälps åt för att hitta möjliga vägar framåt."

**About results:**
> "Deltagarna beskrev att de fick nya verktyg för att arbeta systematiskt och tala strategiskt."

**About quotes:**
> "Jag trodde jag var outcome-driven… tills jag insåg hur snabbt jag börjar prata features."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Too much focus on us:**
"scilla.studio levererade en omfattande utbildning..."
✅ Instead: "Utbildningen gav PMs verktyg för att..."

❌ **Vague claims:**
"Deltagarna blev mer strategiska"
✅ Instead: "PMs formulerade mätbara effektmål och fick feedback på dem"

❌ **Missing context:**
Jumping straight to solution without showing why it was needed
✅ Instead: Build understanding for situation → challenge → method → results

❌ **Buzzword overload:**
"En transformativ resa mot strategiskt ledarskap och produktexcellens"
✅ Instead: "Förflyttningen från Product Owner till Product Manager"

❌ **Only positive quotes:**
All quotes are praise
✅ Instead: Include quotes showing insight, complexity, application

## Final Message

A good case study is not a sales pitch. It's an honest story about how concrete tools and practical training built real capability in real people in a complex situation.

If you can read it and think "this feels authentic" – then you've succeeded.
